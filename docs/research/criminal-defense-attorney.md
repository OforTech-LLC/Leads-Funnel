## CTA Patterns

### Primary CTA Button Text
| High-Converting | Avoid |
|-----------------|-------|
| "Get Legal Help Now" | "Submit" |
| "Speak to an Attorney 24/7" | "Contact" |
| "Free Confidential Consultation" | "Learn More" |
| "Call for Immediate Help" | "Send Message" |
| "Protect Your Rights Today" | "Inquire" |

### CTA Placement Strategy
1. **Hero Section**: Prominent "Call Now" + form
2. **Floating Call Button**: Always visible on mobile
3. **Offense Type Pages**: Specific CTAs per charge type
4. **Exit Intent**: "Get help before you go" popup
5. **After FAQ**: Address remaining hesitation

### Urgency Elements
- "Time is critical. Evidence preservation starts now."
- "Available 24/7 - Including Weekends"
- "Currently Available for Consultation"
- Real-time attorney availability indicator
- "X cases resolved this month"

## Form Field Patterns

### Minimal Form (Fastest Conversion)
```
- Full Name (required)
- Phone Number (required)
- Type of Charge (dropdown)
```

### Standard Intake Form
```
- Full Name (required)
- Phone Number (required)
- Email (required)
- Type of Charge (dropdown: DUI, Drug Offense, Assault, Theft, Other)
- Best Time to Call
```

### Detailed Qualification Form
```
- Full Name
- Phone Number
- Email
- Type of Charge
- Arrest Date (approximate)
- Have you been formally charged? (Yes/No/Not Sure)
- Are you currently in custody? (Yes/No)
- Brief Description (optional)
- Preferred Contact Method
```

### Form Best Practices
- Prioritize phone number capture (urgency of situation)
- Keep initial form to 3-4 fields maximum
- Use dropdown for charge types (helps routing)
- Optional "custody status" helps triage
- Clear confidentiality statement
- Avoid asking for SSN or case details initially

### NACDL Recommended Intake Fields
- Client name (as charged)
- Date of birth
- Arrest date
- Charge(s)
- Court dates (if known)
- Bail status
- Arresting officers' conduct
- Prior criminal history

## Section Structure

### Recommended Page Flow
```
1. HERO SECTION
   - Urgent, action-oriented headline
   - 24/7 availability message
   - Click-to-call + short form
   - Key credentials (former prosecutor, etc.)

2. URGENCY BAR
   - "Time is Critical"
   - 24/7 Available
   - Free Consultation
   - Confidential

3. OFFENSE TYPE NAVIGATION
   - DUI/DWI
   - Drug Offenses
   - Assault/Violence
   - Theft/Property Crimes
   - White Collar
   - Sex Crimes
   - Domestic Violence
   - Federal Crimes
   (Each links to dedicated page)

4. WHY CHOOSE US
   - Former prosecutor experience
   - Local court knowledge
   - Aggressive defense approach
   - Track record of results

5. CASE RESULTS
   - Dismissals
   - Reductions
   - Acquittals
   - Organized by offense type
   - Appropriate disclaimers

6. WHAT TO EXPECT
   - Step 1: Free Consultation
   - Step 2: Case Investigation
   - Step 3: Defense Strategy
   - Step 4: Court Representation
   - Step 5: Resolution

7. ATTORNEY PROFILES
   - Credentials prominently displayed
   - Former prosecutor status
   - Trial experience
   - Professional headshots

8. TESTIMONIALS
   - Video preferred (with consent)
   - Anonymized written reviews
   - Star ratings
   - Outcome mentions

9. FAQ SECTION
   - Address common fears
   - Process questions
   - Cost questions

10. FINAL CTA
    - "Don't Wait. Call Now."
    - Multiple contact options
    - 24/7 emphasis

11. FOOTER
    - Office location
    - Contact info
    - Practice areas
    - Privacy statement
```

- /dui-defense/
- /drug-crimes/
- /assault-battery/
- /theft-crimes/
- /domestic-violence/
- /federal-crimes/

Each page should have:
- Offense-specific headline
- Penalties information
- Defense strategies
- Relevant case results
- Tailored FAQ

### Mobile Optimization
- Click-to-call as primary CTA (critical)
- Sticky call button at all times
- Simplified offense type selection
- Fast page load (stress situation)
- Easy form completion

### Key Metrics to Track
- Call-to-form ratio
- Time to conversion

- Page-to-call conversion

### A/B Testing Priorities
1. Hero headline (urgent vs. reassuring)

## Design Best Practices

### Color Psychology
- **Navy/Dark Blue**: Authority, trust, seriousness
- **Black**: Sophistication, gravity of situation
- **Gold**: Premium service, success
- **White**: Clarity, cleanliness
- **Red (accent only)**: Urgency for CTAs

### Typography
- Bold, authoritative headlines
- Clean, readable body text
- Strong contrast for urgency
- Professional serif or sans-serif

### Imagery Guidelines
- Confident attorney portraits
- Courthouse/legal imagery
- Avoid gavel cliches
- No images implying guilt
- Shield/protection symbolism acceptable

### Tone of Voice
- Confident but not arrogant
- Urgent but not panic-inducing
- Supportive and non-judgmental
- Professional yet accessible
- "Fighting for you" language

### Page Speed
- Critical (people in crisis)
- Optimize all images
- Minimal scripts
- Fast mobile load
- Target under 2 seconds
